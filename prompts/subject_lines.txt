Generate 5 subject lines for a cold outreach email to {company_name}.

{agency_analysis}

============================================================
SUBJECT LINE STRATEGY
============================================================
Goal: highest open rate. Each line must feel written for {company_name} only.

PERSONALIZATION (required):
- At least 3 of 5 lines MUST include {company_name} (or a natural short form, e.g. "Goji" for Goji Labs).
- Anchor each line to something specific from the agency analysis: a service, client type, stack, niche, or workflow — not just "AI" or "marketing."
- Never send a line that could apply unchanged to 100 other agencies.

CURIOSITY & PSYCHOLOGY:
- Use open loops — tease an insight without revealing it in the subject.
- Pattern interrupts beat predictable cold-email formulas.
- Micro-personalization, subtle FOMO, and peer tone work. Hype and manipulation do not.
- Questions and incomplete thoughts are fine. End without a period.

BANNED — overused, predictable, or spammy (never use these or close variants):
- "something i noticed on your site"
- "noticed something on your site"
- "quick question"
- "following up"
- "ai engineer open to contract"
- "exciting opportunity"
- "partnership opportunity"
- "free", "guarantee", "limited time", "act now", "urgent"

STYLE RULES:
- 4 to 10 words per line (short enough for mobile, long enough to be specific).
- Sentence case. No emojis. No trailing period.
- No title case. No ALL CAPS.

INSPIRATION — adapt the intent, do NOT copy verbatim:
- "saw something on {company_name}'s site — wild idea inside"
- "quick thought for {company_name}'s product team"
- "what most agencies miss (but {company_name} could nail)"
- "{company_name} and a gap on your homepage"
- "if i were at {company_name}, i'd want to know this"
- "one micro-idea for {company_name} — not a pitch"
- "how {company_name} could ship AI faster"
- "re {company_name}'s client onboarding stack"
- "your [service from analysis] workflow — quick thought"

VARY the angle across all 5 lines:
1. Website or product-specific observation (open loop)
2. Industry or client-niche hook from their analysis
3. Stack or workflow reference (Python, n8n, LLM, etc. if in analysis)
4. Peer question that implies you understand their world
5. Bold but honest curiosity hook with company name

Rank lines by specificity to THIS agency before outputting.
The best line for selection is the most specific, not the most generic.
